MLB Player Props

Juan Soto to Record an RBI (+125)

The New York Yankees and the Philadelphia Phillies are among the five best records in baseball and will begin a three-game series on Monday. The series starts with heavy hitters on the mound as Luis Gil (3.10 ERA) and Zack Wheeler (2.55 ERA) will be on the bump.

Juan Soto is one of the Yanks' only batters with past success against Wheeler. The two are very familiar with each other, totaling 48 head-to-head appearances as former divisional rivals. Soto is carrying a .250 average and .410 OBP against Wheeler, who holds +150 odds to win the National League Cy Young award.

New York's star outfielder has solid RBI production over his previous seven contests, racking up eight ribbies. Soto has also totaled 3 home runs among his 12 career hits against Wheeler. All it could take is one crack of the bat for Soto to bat in a run. The four-time All-Star has four dingers over his last six.

While Soto smacking a long ball (+320) carries juicy odds, I'm taking the "safer" bet with an RBI. The production has been very consistent over his last seven contests (1.1 per game).

Willson Contreras to Record 2+ Total Bases (+125)

No one has been rolling like Willson Contreras in the St. Louis Cardinals' batting order. He's reached at least two total bases in three straight games, including a homer in back-to-back games.

The opportunity is present for Contreras to keep it up against the Texas Rangers' Nathan Eovaldi. While the Rangers' starter has mostly provided solid starts in July with a 3.00 ERA over five appearances, he's far from invincible. In fact, Eovaldi just let up eight hits and six earned runs on July 19th.

Contreras has been raking against four-seam fastballs (.323) and splitters (.400) in 2024. Guess which pitches are Eovaldi's go-to choices? Yep, a four seamer (36.7%) and splitter (30.8%).

Paul Skenes Over 7.5 Strikeouts (+110)

Among starting pitchers with at least 50 innings pitched, Paul Skenes leads baseball with a 2.40 SIERA and 2.27 xFIP. The strikeout numbers are just as good with Skenes holding a 34.2% strikeout percentage (K%) and 11.69 K/9 -- both marks are the second-highest among starters.

Skenes is logging 8.1 Ks per game on the season and 9.0 in July. It's hard to pass on over 7.5 strikeouts carrying plus odds.

The Astros sitting outside the top 10 in runs above average against sliders only helps Skenes' case. His slider touts a 42.9% strikeout rate with opponents carrying an expected batting average (xBA) of .126.
